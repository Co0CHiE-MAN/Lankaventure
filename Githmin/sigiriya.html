The Ancient Lion Fortress
Sigiriya
Sigiriya ෴ සීගිරිය

An ancient stronghold and palace called Sigiriya, sometimes referred to as Lion Rock, is situated in Sri Lanka's central Matale District. It was constructed by King Kashyapa in the 5th century AD and is perched atop a 660-foot (200-meter) tall granite formation. Beautiful water gardens, moats, and terraced gardens line the perimeter of the stronghold, which is thought to be one of Sri Lanka's best examples of ancient urban planning. One of the oldest surviving specimens of Sri Lankan art can be found in a protected nook of the rock face in Sigiriya, which is also well-known for its frescoes. .

Significant murals of stunning, partially-naked ladies are found throughout the Sigiriya compound and are supposed to be either representations of the king's harem or celestial nymphs. A huge pair of lion's paws carved into the rock face that served as an entryway to the palace complex is another feature. A series of mirrored wall murals with inscriptions and poetry are also there. As one of the most well-known tourist locations in Sri Lanka today and a UNESCO World Heritage site, Sigiriya draws tourists from all over the world who come to marvel at its historical and cultural significance.
